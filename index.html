A Little About Passover

Passover or Pesach (פֶּסַח‎) is a major Jewish holiday and one of the most widely celebrated Jewish holidays. Together with Shavuot and Sukkot, Passover was one of the Three Pilgrimage Festivals during which the entire population of the kingdom of Judah made a pilgrimage to the Temple in Jerusalem. Samaritans still make this pilgrimage to Mount Gerizim, but only men participate in public worship.

How long is Passover?

Passover is 8 days longs. It starts after the sunsets (this year it begins the evening of Wednesday, April 7th) and ends after sunset 8 days later (this year it ends the evening of Thursday, April 16).

What Can You Not Eat During Passover?

    Anything with...
  • Wheat
  • Barley
  • Rye
  • Oats
  • Spelt

What Must be on the Sedar Plate during the Pesach Sedar?

  1. Z'roa: roasted lamb shank bone
  2. Beitzah: a roasted or hard-boiled egg
  3. Maror: a bitter herb
  4. Chazeret: a second bitter herb
  5. Charoset: a sweet fruit mixture made with wine, honey or nuts
  6. Karpas: a green vegetable
